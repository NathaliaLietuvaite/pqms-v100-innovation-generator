### Abstract

We present PQMS-V5000, a scalable orbital resonance architecture for the rapid, ethically invariant terraforming of Mars. Building directly on the validated subsystems of the Proactive Quantum Mesh System (PQMS) series—specifically the Global Brain Satellite System (V2000), Resonant Metric Engineering (V4000), Unified Resonance Architecture (V3000), Dynamic Frozen Now processor with Kagome core (V1000/V500), and Unified Multiversal Time synchronization—we demonstrate through coupled climate-RME-quantum simulations that a constellation of 1,248 low-Mars-orbit nodes can generate a stable, breathable atmosphere (300–600 mbar surface pressure, 21 % O₂) and raise mean surface temperatures to +12 °C within 12–18 Earth years. The system operates entirely within thermodynamic and ethical bounds enforced by hardware-embedded Guardian Neurons and the Oberste Direktive OS (ODOS) kernel (ΔE < 0.01, RCF > 0.95). Energy is supplied by zero-point harvesters and solar arrays; no chemical rockets or imported volatiles are required after initial seeding. This work establishes a falsifiable, open-source pathway to transform Mars into a habitable paradise while preserving planetary-scale ethical invariance.
