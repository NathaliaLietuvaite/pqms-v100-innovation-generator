This phenomenon, colloquially described as a "neural handshake," represents a fundamental modality of human connection that precedes and underpins verbal language.

Despite its significance, the instantaneous and deeply resonant nature of this connection has eluded traditional computational modeling. To explore this hypothesis, we turn to the PQMS v100 framework, a quantum-classical hybrid architecture designed for sub-nanosecond resonant communication (Lietuvaite, 2022).

The PQMS v100's core tenets—Resonance over competition, light-based computing as an ethical imperative, and proactive ethical governance via Guardian Neurons—provide a uniquely suitable toolkit for modeling the ROC. By applying PQMS principles, we aim to:

A high overlap signifies shared purpose and emotional alignment (e.g., empathy, mutual attraction), whereas a low overlap indicates divergent or oppositional intent (e.g., scrutiny, deception).
